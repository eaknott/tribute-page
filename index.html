Ruth Bader Ginsburg

The woman on whose shoulders we stand

A timeline of Ruth Bader Ginsburg's life:

  • 1933 - Joan Ruth Bader is born in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1950 - Enrolls at Cornell University.
  • 1954 - Graduates from Cornell University. She marries Marty Ginsburg at his family's home.
  • 1955 - Gives birth to a daughter, Jane Ginsburg.
  • 1956 - Enrolls in Harvard Law School as one of only nine women in her class.
  • 1957 - In her second year of law school, her husband Marty is diagnosed with cancer.
  • 1958 - Transfers to Columbia University School of Law. Her husband, Marty, graduates from Harvard Law School.
  • 1959 - Graduates from Columbia Law at the top of her class, yet has tremendous difficulty landing a job.
  • 1963 - Becomes the second woman to teach full-time at Rutgers School of Law.
  • 1965 - Publishes her first book, Civil Procedure in Sweden, with Anders Bruzelius. Her son, James Ginsburg, is born.
  • 1970 - Teaches her first class on women and the law.
  • 1971 - Writes her first brief to the Supreme Court in Reed v. Reed.
  • 1972 - Becomes the first female tenured professor at Columbia Law School. She also cofounds the Women's Rights Project at the American Civil Liberties Union.
  • 1974 - Publishes the first-ever casebook on sex discrimination. She insists that the author's names be listed alphabetically, even though doing so means the one man's name will come first.
  • 1980 - President Jimmy Carter nominates Ginsburg to the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit.
  • 1993 - President Bill Clinton nominates Ginsburg to be an associate justice of the United States Supreme Court.
  • 1996 - Writes the majority opinion in the landmark case United States v. Virginia, requiring the Virginia Military Institute to admit women.
  • 1999 - Ginsburg is diagnosed with colorectal cancer. She does not miss a day on the bench.
  • 2000 - Ginsburg is one of four dissenters in Bush v. Gore, which effectively declares George W. Bush president.
  • 2007 - Ginsburg launches her era of furious dissent with the abortion case Gonzales v. Carhart. She also reads her dissent from the bench in the sex discrimination case of Lilly Ledbetter.
  • 2000 - Ginsburg has surgery to remove a cancerous tumor. Within 3 weeks, she is back on the bench, and she attends President Obama's first speech to Congress.
  • 2010 - Ginsburg's husband, Marty, dies after complications from metastatic cancer.
  • 2013 - Ginsburg becomes the first Supreme Court justice to officiate a same-sex wedding. As she dissents in a case gutting the Voting Rights Act, Notorious R.B.G., the viral Tumblr, is born.
  • 2015 - Admits that she as not "100 percent sober" at the State of the Union. She becomes a recurring character on Saturday Night Live's Weekend Update, issuing "Ginsburns" between dance moves.
  • 2020 - Dies from complications of pancreatic cancer in her home in Washington, D.C., at the age of 87.
